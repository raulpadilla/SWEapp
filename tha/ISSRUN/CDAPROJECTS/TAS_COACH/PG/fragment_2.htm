A Fragment Strategy divides the opportunity into smaller pieces, and focuses the customer on a subset of the issues that you can address. When using a fragment strategy, you attempt to focus on the portion of the opportunity you think you can win.

Guidelines for implementing a fragment strategy are:

  • Developing leverage for the future. The idea is to achieve a foothold or presence that you can expand later. So, make sure to choose a base from which you can expand for future revenue (#17).
  • Obtaining insider support. To attain leverage in an organization, you need support from the people who want you to win.

Potential problems include:

  • Expending resources on a limited opportunity. Be sure you are using your resources wisely, since the fragment you choose will not be as profitable as the whole opportunity. Monitor the cost of sales (#18) to be sure your investment is in line with the potential long-term rewards.
  • Opening the playing field to additional competitors. When you pursue one specific piece of the opportunity, other competitors may imitate your strategy.
