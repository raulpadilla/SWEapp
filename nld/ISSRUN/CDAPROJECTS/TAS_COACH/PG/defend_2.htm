A Defend Strategy protects your position from inevitable assault by competitors. When using a defend strategy, you attempt to protect your position with the customer.

Guidelines for implementing a defend strategy are:

  • Extend your relationship (#12). Use your presence in the account to build relationships at the executive level, thus ensuring that you are the first to hear about new business initiatives and opportunities.
  • Support your allies. Your allies at every level can help you fend off competitive intrusions. Make sure you support them when they need it, so they will support you when the time comes.
  • Articulate your credibility (#12) and business value (#10). Consider having a review with the key players in the account. Use the sessions to reinforce your credibility, and get credit for the value your solutions brings to the organization.

Potential problems include:

  • Self-Isolation. There is a tendency to relax after you have closed an opportunity. Don't allow yourself to get too comfortable and stop expanding your relationship..
  • Complacency. Be careful not to let that victory blind you to your competitors' attacks, which will come.
