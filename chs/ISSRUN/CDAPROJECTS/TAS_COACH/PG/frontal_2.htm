A Frontal Strategy is a direct approach based on the customers perception of your overwhelming superiority in solution, price, or reputation.

Guidelines for implementing a frontal strategy are:

  • A 3:1 advantage. The customer must perceive that you are three times better than your competition in product or service functions, sales resources, price or other critical selection criteria.
  • Strength, speed, or surprise. Using a frontal strategy requires overwhelming strength, ability to close the sale before the competition can react, or being first on the scene (so you can set the agenda).

Potential problems include:

  • Resource requirements (#8). Using this strategy requires lots of time, money and personnel because you have to overwhelm the competition.
  • Obvious to competition. The competition will see what you are doing immediately, so you have to act quickly; otherwise the competition might have an opportunity to outmaneuver you.
  • Easiest to defeat. Because of the cautions mentioned above, the frontal strategy is the most easily defeated strategy. It is also the most common strategy used by sellers.
